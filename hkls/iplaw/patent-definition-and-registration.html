Patent (I): Definition and Registration

What is Patent?

Why is this relevant to you?

Most Patents are a set of exclusive property rights granted to inventors, provided that these inventions satisfy the legal requirements. Business starters often come up with an interesting product or idea to start their business, it is therefore important for them to understand the nature of patent and register their inventions as soon as practicable to gain protection under the law.

Understanding and the registration of a patent would be crucial to business starters/owners of invention in 2 main aspects:

  • Protection against infringement
  • Generating substantial revenue by way of licensing (and the regularly collected fees known as “royalties”)

Are all inventions registrable?

It is almost certain that not all inventions can be registered as patent. To register an invention as patent, there are 2 main questions to be asked.

1) Is the subject matter of the invention patentable? (“Patentable subject matter”)

2) Are the substantive standards of patent registration satisfied? (“3 substantive standards”)

    a. Is it new in the industry?

    b. Does creating the invention involve an inventive step?

    c. Is the invention susceptible of industrial application?

This article will deal with the first question and procedures of registration. The 3 substantive standards will be dealt with in the second article “Patent (II): Infringement & Protection”.

Territoriality of Patents

Despite that globalization has fostered technological advancement with similar pace around the world, and that similar inventions elsewhere may affect the prospect of one’s patent application in Hong Kong, the scope of protection of patent registration in Hong Kong applies to Hong Kong only.

Therefore, it is advised that owners register their patents also in other jurisdictions such as the intended place of manufacture, distribution and even transit. Application in Hong Kong or most jurisdictions (those under Paris Convention) grants priority right to the owner, i.e. the right to claim the application date of patent filings in the later applications as the application date of the first registration within 12 months, to “take priority” over other potential patent applications in other jurisdictions.

Patentable subject matter

It is possible to patent both the product and process of the invention. That means, if the inventor has invented a new and crucial component in a car, both the component and the technological, procedural specification of how to make that component can be registered. But of course, the owner has to consider to file either the process claim or the product claim when they actually refer to the same invention (concerning the 3 substantive standards).

Instead of stating directly what is patentable, the law states the subject matter that are excluded from patent, they are:

(i) Discovery, scientific theory or mathematical method A discovery consists of something that already exist in the nature world, so it is really finding out something that is already existing rather than inventing it, and thus it is excluded (e.g. an ore or mineral). Similarly, scientific theory such as the discovery of gravity will also not be patentable
(ii) Aesthetic creation Aesthetic creations are usually protected by registered design or copyright.
(iii) Scheme, rule or method for performing mental act, playing a game or doing business or computer program A computer program is usually protected by copyright, while a business scheme would normally be protected under Trade Secrets.
(iv) Presentation of information The method of presenting information, such as the electronic film to present the patterns and sequences of genes in human body is not patentable.
(v) Plant/animal variety/ essential biological process

The exclusion concerns with natural, sexual biological process among animal and plant varieties, but the exclusion does not apply to microbiological process or product of such process.

The exclusion is mainly upheld on the basis of public morality, on the basis of potentially negatives effects to the species by testing and genetic engineering.
(vi) Surgery, therapy, diagnostic method

The exclusion does not apply to therapeutic and cosmetic procedures, as well as the product of the method, provided that the manufacturing of that product does not require a surgical step, measure or diagnosis.

The exclusion is upheld on the basis that a treatment to human/animals should not be prohibited from widely adopting by patents.1

Exclusion of inventions concerning categories (i) to (iv) is not absolute. Inventions falling into these categories can still be registered, satisfying the qualification of “technological contribution”.2 For instance, the electronic film presenting gene sequences is not patentable, but the method of isolating a gene (rather than in its naturally existing state) is patentable. The same applies to computer programs, those providing a technical solution to problems (e.g. increasing resolution of old photos) is patentable.

Patent Registration

There are two kinds of patent in Hong Kong:

Short term patent Long term/Standard patent
Length of protection (renewal period included) 8 years 20 years
Application procedures (Please see below) Formality check only Must first apply in 3 designated patent office

*Important note:

Patent registration in Hong Kong conducts only formality check, i.e. whether the required forms and fees have been properly filled and paid respectively. Substantive checks concerning patentable subject matter and the 3 substantive standards are not conducted.

For long term patents, it is assumed that the substantive checks are done in the designated patent office while this is not the case for short term patents. Therefore, successful registration in Hong Kong as a short term patent does not mean the patent is valid and free from infringement claims.

Short term patent

To make a short term patent registration, the owner of the invention has to file the documents and pay fees as listed below.

Required filing documents
  • a request for grant on Patents Form P6;
  • a specification which includes: (i) a description of the invention to which the application relates; (ii) one or more claims but not exceeding one independent claim; (iii) any drawings referred to in the description or the claim or claims;
    • To see what should be included in the description, claims, drawings please refer to Patent (General) Rules Section 59, 60 and 64.
  • an abstract in both English and Chinese (please see Patent (General) Rules Section 61 for details required);
  • the title of invention in both English and Chinese;
  • applicant's name and address;
  • inventor's name and address;
  • a search report
    • a search report is a report by a prescribed searching authority of a search undertaken by that authority as to the prior art in relation to the invention
    • For contents needed to be included in the search report please see Patent (General) Rules Section 72
  • if you are not the inventor, a statement on Patents Form P6A indicating your entitlement to the short-term patent;
  • if priority is claimed, a statement of priority and priority documents (please see Patent (General) Rules Section 69);
    • Priority right is the right you have if you have file an application for the same invention in any other country that is a member country to the Paris Convention, you can claim the early filing date as filing date for later application if you file the later within 12 months.
  • if non-prejudicial disclosure is claimed, a statement and written evidence to support the claim (Patent (General) Rules Section 70 for written evidence that is required); (Refer to “Novelty” in “Patent(II): Infringement and Protection” article for meaning of non-prejudicial disclosure)
  • an address for service in Hong Kong, China;
  • translations of information and documents as required;
  • address of the patent agent in Hong Kong (if applicable).
Required fees
  • A filing fee (HK$ 755) and;
  • An advertisement fee (HK$ 68)

To be paid within one month of the earliest filing of any part of the application. Please refer to http://www.ipd.gov.hk/eng/forms_fees/patents.htm for the updated fees.

Standard patent

Application for a standard patent involves a more sophisticated application procedure.

Preliminary stage

To apply for a standard patent, the applicant will first need to apply for a patent in the three designated patent office:

  • the State Intellectual Property Office, People's Republic of China;
  • the European Patent Office, in respect of a patent designating the United Kingdom
  • the United Kingdom Patent Office
    • Stage One

    The applicant must file a request to record in Hong Kong within six months after the publication of the designated patent application in the designated patent office listed above.

    The documents needed for the application are:

    • a request to record on Patents Form P4;
    • a copy of the published designated patent application;
    • the title of the invention in both English and Chinese;
    • an abstract in both English and Chinese;
    • applicant's name and address;
    • if you are not the applicant named in the designated patent application, a statement explaining your right to apply and supporting documents (for example a copy of an assignment);
    • if the inventor is not named in the designated patent application, a statement identifying the person you believe is the inventor;
    • If you claimed priority, you need to state the name of the country or territory where the priority application was filed, the priority application number and the priority application filing date;
    • If you claimed non-prejudicial disclosure, you need to state the name and place of the prescribed exhibition or meeting at which the invention was disclosed, the opening date of the exhibition or meeting, and the date of first disclosure;
    • an address for service in Hong Kong, China;
    • translations of information and documents as required;
    • Address of the patent agent in Hong Kong (if applicable).

    The required fees are:

    • a filing fee (HK$380)
    • an advertisement fee (HK$68)

    to be paid within one month of the earliest filing of any part of your request to record. Please refer to http://www.ipd.gov.hk/eng/forms_fees/patents.htm for the updated fees.

    Stage Two

    After publication of the request to record in Hong Kong or the grant of the designated patent by the designated patent office (whichever is later), the applicant must file a request for registration and grant in Hong Kong within six months.

    The documents needed are:

    • a request for registration and grant on Patents Form P5;
    • a copy of the published specification of the designated patent (you need to confirm that it is a true copy of the document issued by or kept at the relevant designated patent office);
    • the title of invention in both English and Chinese;
    • if you are not the applicant named in the standard patent application, a statement explaining your right to apply and supporting documents which establish your right (for example a copy of an assignment);
    • an address for service in Hong Kong, China;
    • translations of information and documents as required;
    • Address of the patent agent in Hong Kong (if applicable)

    The required fees are:

    • A filing fee (HK$380)
    • An advertisement fee (HK$68)

    To be paid within one month of the earliest filing of any part of your request for registration and grant. Please refer to http://www.ipd.gov.hk/eng/forms_fees/patents.htm for the updated fees.

    Renewal

    Application does not automatically render the two types of patent protection for duration as stated above. Patent owners are required to renew their patent application and pay fees for the renewal to gain the longest duration of protection in Hong Kong.
